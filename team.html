The Tiny Sun Worshipers

Tiny Sun would have never turned out that well without the great project team behind it. A community of fate thrown together based on their differing backgrounds. Scroll down to learn more about each team member's skills and responsibilities.

Manasa Sama

An electrical engineer by training, Manasa contributed in the early stages of the project with a multitude of exploratory sketches that proved her vivid imagination. As we started prototyping, she experimented fearlessly with carboard and PLA and noticed the komorebi in one of GIX's offices for the first time. Later, she contributed with her great Fusion 360 skills and took responsibility for most of our bazaar display, including the poster and the animation of the clockwork powering the moving parts of our final prototypes.

Jack Bowen

Jack, a true Jack of all trades, shone with his vast experience in fabrication and mechanics. Not only did he contribute to the final prototype with great ideas all along the way, but he also acted as our true north when it came to judging what we can and what we can not build in the timeframe given to us. Beyond engineering and constructing the inner workings of our final prototype and producing the final video, Jack taught Manasa and Sebastian the essentials of woodworking, a valuable skill for future endeavors.

Sebastian Kuhn

As a newcomer to the world of hardware and fabrication, Sebastian tried his best to learn from his teammates and contributed by experimenting with various materials. With his analytical thinking, he contributed to making sure that the team stayed on the path to success and to making the most out of the instructors' feedback, pivoting to new ideas when necessary. He tried to make himself useful in the woodshop and assembly of our final prototype, learning great new skills, and contributed further by building our portfolio website.
